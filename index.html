🗺️ Sligo Territory

Everything on
your doorstep

Sligo is one of Ireland's best-kept secrets. Mountains, waterfalls, ancient stones and wild Atlantic coast — all within 30 minutes of the house.

Knocknarea  ·  Benbulben  ·  Strandhill
Glencar Waterfall  ·  Mullaghmore
Lough Gill  ·  Aughris Head
Carrowmore  ·  Rosses Point
Sligo Abbey  ·  Lissadell House
Streedagh Beach  ·  Gleniff Horseshoe
🏔️ Mountains & Forests
Iconic Hike
Knocknarea — Queen Maeve's Trail
Panoramic coastal views. An ancient queen's cairn. One of the best hikes in all of Ireland.
Forest Walk
Benbulben Forest Walk
Gentle trails beneath Sligo's famous flat-topped mountain. Magical on a misty morning.
Lake & Woodland
Hazelwood — Lough Gill
Beautiful family walks along the lake that inspired Yeats. Peaceful and close to town.
Hidden Valley
Gleniff Horseshoe
A secret valley completely ringed by mountains. Feels like discovering something nobody else knows.
💦 Waterfalls
Easy Walk
Glencar Waterfall
Short and stunning. Great for all ages.
Seasonal Spectacle
Devil's Chimney
Ireland's tallest waterfall — only flows after heavy rain. Worth timing your visit around it.
🏖️ Beaches & Coast
Surf Beach
Strandhill
Wild and windswept. Brilliant for surfing or golden-hour dune walks.
Calm & Family
Rosses Point
Calmer water, gorgeous coastal walk, views of Benbulben from the shore.
Atlantic Drama
Mullaghmore Head
Dramatic cliffs and crashing waves. One of the most spectacular spots in Ireland.
Hidden Gem
Aughris Head Loop
Sligo's best-kept coastal secret. Almost no crowds.
Historic Shipwreck
Streedagh Beach
A vast, wild beach with Spanish Armada wreck history and dramatic dune walks.
🏛️ History & Culture
5,000 Years Old
Carrowmore Megalithic Cemetery
One of Ireland's largest ancient tomb complexes. Quietly extraordinary.
Town Centre
Sligo Abbey
A beautiful 13th-century ruin right in the heart of Sligo town.
Stately Home
Lissadell House & Gardens
Georgian country house and gardens with deep ties to the Gore-Booth family and Irish history.
Literary Pilgrimage
Yeats' Grave, Drumcliffe
The final resting place of W.B. Yeats, beneath Benbulben — exactly where he wished to be.
Local Culture
Sligo Farmers Market
Saturday mornings in town — local cheese, baked goods, crafts and a proper sense of community.
🍽️ Dining

Where to eat —
the best in town

A selection of Sligo's most highly rated restaurants, based on consistent praise across reviews. From fine dining to the best burger in town.

Eala Bhán★★★★★
Rockwood Parade, Sligo Town
Fine dining with exquisite seafood and steak in a charming riverside setting. Consistently rated among the very best in Sligo.
Special Occasion
Coach Lane at Donaghy's★★★★★
Lord Edward Street, Sligo Town
A Sligo institution since the mid-1990s. Dry-aged Irish Angus steak, a tomahawk for two, and an extensive wine list with an on-site sommelier.
Steak & Seafood
Hooked★★★★★
Garavogue River, Sligo Town
Seasonal Irish produce with a menu that changes regularly. Brunch here is a genuine highlight, especially at weekends.
Brunch & Seafood
Flipside★★★★★
Rockwood Parade, Sligo Town
The best burger in Sligo, hands down. Locally sourced beef, proper fries, and "The Dictator" fried chicken burger is a signature dish.
Casual & Burgers
Bistro Bianconi★★★★★
Sligo Town
A little piece of Italy in Sligo. Properly authentic pasta and pizza, generous portions, and a warm atmosphere.
Italian
Stoked★★★★★
Shore Road, Strandhill
A genuine hidden gem above the Strand Bar. Globally-inspired sharing tapas — try the Korean cauliflower wings and the haddock bao bun. Often called the best restaurant in Sligo.
Tapas & Sharing
Otto★★★★★
Sligo Town
A stylish wine bar and pizzeria with a wood-fired Valoriani oven at its heart. 48-hour proofed Neapolitan pizza, excellent small plates, and a beautifully curated wine list.
Pizza & Wine Bar
Davis' Restaurant & Yeats Tavern★★★★☆
Drumcliffe, just outside Sligo Town
Family-run for over 35 years. Classic Irish cooking — slow-cooked beef, fresh Atlantic fish, and excellent Sunday roasts.
Traditional Irish
The Driftwood★★★★☆
Rosses Point
Sea views over Sligo Bay with a relaxed, family-friendly setup. Wonderful spot for a sunset dinner.
Seaside Dining
Hargadons Pub★★★★☆
O'Connell Street, Sligo Town
A historic Victorian pub serving hearty, proper pub food in beautifully preserved surroundings.
Traditional Pub
Lyons Cafe★★★★☆
Sligo Town
A Sligo classic for coffee, cake, and lunch. Lovely rooftop terrace seating in good weather.
Café & Lunch
Rugantino★★★★★
Sligo Town
The newest favourite in town — small, intimate, and quickly becoming a local go-to for excellent Italian food.
Italian
🛒 Shops & Supermarkets

Stock the kitchen
like a local

Sligo town has every major supermarket within a few minutes' drive. Here's where locals actually go for specific things.

Supermarket
Location
Best For
Tesco
O'Connell Street, Town Centre
Biggest range overall — fresh fish counter, click & collect, latest opening hours (until 10pm).
Dunnes Stores
Cranmore Road
Fresh meat & fish counter, plus a solid bakery section.
Lidl
Finisklin Road / Cranmore Rd
Best value for everyday groceries. Open latest of all (8am–10pm).
Aldi
Cranmore Road
Great for wine & specials — their weekly "Specialbuys" are worth a browse.
Kate's Kitchen
Castle Street, Town Centre
A Sligo institution — artisan deli goods, local cheese, and great for a proper picnic hamper.
Sligo Farmers Market
Town Centre · Saturdays only
The best spot for local produce, fresh bread and artisan goods — Saturday mornings only.

💡 Local tip: for the freshest fish, ask at the counter early in the day, or visit on Fridays when deliveries tend to arrive.
